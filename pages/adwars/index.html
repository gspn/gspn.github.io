The Basics

Winning the ad wars on youtube is already simple enough. For one, the godsends at uBlock are regularly updating their adblock filters to bypass whatever spy-gadgety observer youtube has for detecting them.
My personal setup uses fadblock (see edit) + adnauseam. The one who requested this article uses fadblock + brave shield. What do these do and what are their pros and cons? Glad you asked!

Personal reccomendations

This part is quite short but also descriptive.

Firstly, fadblock is functionally an adskipper, fast-forwarding the media element of video ads on youtube.

    + Youtube can't detect it. Ha!
    + Plays well with other blockers
    - Some hitches (ads might play for half a second)

(edit: fadblock is no longer necessay, uBo does a better job. also it vaguely has some malware-like stuff in it since about 2024)

And secondly, adnauseam is based on uBlock Origin and obfuscates whatever data the ads can still get before they're nuked by spamming requests in the background. To my understanding, this may also register the reward at the ad service, which helps creators (although youtube finds every way to take that revenue away).

    + Based on uBo
    + Messes with trackers
    - Uses more bandwidth

I won't go into brave shield, since it's not an extension or script and that's a topic for the browser debate.
